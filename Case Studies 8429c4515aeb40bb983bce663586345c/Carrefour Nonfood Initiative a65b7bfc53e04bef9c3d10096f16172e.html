Carrefour: Nonfood Initiative

Tags Carrefour
TLDR Carrefour implemented a Non-Food Initiative to boost non-food sales in the digital channel. We developed a new tab-based navigation system, conducted A/B testing, and created separate user journeys for different product categories. The initiative resulted in a 25% increase in gross merchandise value (GMV) for non-food categories. Key learnings include the importance of dedicated user journeys, A/B testing, cross-functional collaboration, and continuous monitoring of key metrics. Next steps include further optimizing the non-food user journey, implementing additional features, and maximizing overall business performance.

Carrefour Non-Food Initiative A/B Test Case Study

Background

Carrefour, is a major player in the e-commerce space, and we were facing challenges with our non-food product sales in the digital channel. Despite a strong presence in offline stores, the share of non-food sales was significantly lower in the digital space.

Problem Statement

Our User research indicated that Carrefour was primarily perceived as a grocery brand in digital channels. This perception was reinforced by the limited space allocated to non-food assortment in primary merchandising real estate on the platform.

Objectives

  1. Boost the Non-Food Business by creating a separate user journey on consumer-facing apps.
  1. Increase revenue and Total Contribution Margin (TCM) for Non-Food products by improving discoverability.
  1. Maintain or increase revenue, Average Order Value (AOV), and TCM for Grocery products.

Approach

  1. Develop a new tab-based navigation system to give dedicated real estate to Non-Food products.
  1. Implement A/B testing to validate the effectiveness of the new design.
  1. Create separate user journeys for Scheduled Grocery, Now (express delivery), and Non-Food products.
  1. Enhance the Content Management System (CMS) to support the new tab-based structure.
  1. Optimize search functionality and product discovery for each tab.

Implementation

  1. Designed and developed a 3-tab navigation system:
    • Tab 1: Now (Express delivery)
    • Tab 2: Scheduled (All products) or Scheduled Grocery
    • Tab 3: Electronics+ (Non-Food products)
  1. Created new variations of home pages, category navigation, and deals pages for each tab.
  1. Implemented A/B testing using GrowthBook with four variations:
    • Control: Old 2-tab design (10% of traffic)
    • Variation A: New 3-tab design (10% of traffic)
    • Rest of 80% of the customers are not included in the A/B test
  1. Enhanced the CMS to support tab components and new content requirements.
  1. Optimized search functionality, including autosuggest and search results, to align with the selected tab.
  1. Implemented changes to the Buy Again experience, onboarding design, and deep linking to support the new structure.

What It Looks Like

Results

The A/B test for the Non-Food Initiative validated the hypothesis that improving non-food category discoverability could increase their GMV contribution. Initial rollout to 10% of users resulted in a 18% GMV increase for these categories, exceeding the projected 15% improvement.

Key Learnings

  1. Dedicated user journeys for different product categories can significantly impact discoverability and sales.
  1. A/B testing is crucial for validating hypotheses and measuring the impact of major UX changes.
  1. Cross-functional collaboration (Product, Design, Engineering, Marketing) is essential for successful implementation of complex initiatives.
  1. Continuous monitoring of key metrics (Revenue, TCM, AOV) across all product categories is necessary to ensure overall business growth.

Next Steps

  1. Analyze user behavior and feedback from the A/B test to further optimize the non-food user journey.
  1. Consider implementing fast-follower features, such as introducing filters for Food, Non-Food, and Now on the Buy Again page.
  1. Explore opportunities to enhance the progress bar feature for Minimum Order Value and Free Shipping Threshold.
  1. Continue to monitor and optimize the balance between food and non-food product discovery to maximize overall business performance.
