Carrefour: Express only experimentation in Jordan

TagsCarrefour
TLDRCarrefour's experiment in Jordan to discontinue Scheduled delivery services and focus solely on NOW express delivery aimed to increase revenue but resulted in a 13% overall sales decrease. The experiment revealed that completely hiding non-express products negatively impacted sales, with varying effects across different stores. Key learnings include the importance of maintaining product visibility and flexible delivery options to balance express delivery benefits with non-food and scheduled delivery revenue. The findings will refine the Non-Food Initiative and suggest a hybrid approach for market-specific strategies and improved user experience.

Background

Following the 2024 Goals of improving profitability in all markets, Carrefour identified another challenge in specific markets: Jordan, Oman, and Kuwait were experiencing revenue losses due to their Scheduled delivery proposition.

Problem Statement

The Scheduled delivery service in Jordan, Oman, and Kuwait was negatively impacting revenue. Country teams proposed discontinuing the Scheduled delivery service and focusing solely on the NOW express delivery service to address this issue.

Objectives

  1. Increase NOW delivery service revenue to >15% in the 3 affected markets.
  1. Maintain sales from non-food categories.
  1. Evaluate the impact of closing Scheduled deliveries on overall revenue and customer experience.

Approach

  1. Conduct an experiment to determine the impact of closing Scheduled deliveries in these markets.
  1. Focus on Jordan as the primary test market.
  1. Implement changes to the e-commerce platform to support the experiment.
  1. Monitor key metrics to assess the impact of the changes.

Implementation

  1. In Jordan, closed down the Scheduled services, showing only the Express homepage to customers.
  1. Removed non-express products from search results.
  1. Prevented customers from adding scheduled products to their carts.

Key Metrics Monitored

  • NOW GMV
  • NOW add to cart rate
  • Average Order Value (AOV) for NOW
  • Overall sales impact

Results

The experiment yielded mixed results:

  1. Positive Outcomes:
    • Solved the issue of broken experiences when products were unavailable for specific locations.
  1. Negative Outcomes:
    • Total sales decreased by 13% across all stores in Jordan.
    • Significant variation in impact across stores:
      • Al Madina: -37%
      • Amman City Mall: +2%
      • Irbid City Center: -1%
      • Zarqa Store: -8%

Key Learnings

  1. Completely hiding non-express products, regardless of availability, negatively impacted overall sales.
  1. The experiment validated the approach taken in the Non-Food Initiative, which aims to show available products under different delivery services.
  1. A more nuanced approach is needed to balance the benefits of express delivery with the revenue potential of non-food and scheduled delivery products.

Connection to Non-Food Initiative

This experiment provides valuable insights that complement and refine the Non-Food Initiative:

  1. Visibility of Non-Food Products: The negative impact of completely hiding non-express products reinforces the importance of the Non-Food Initiative's goal to improve non-food product visibility.
  1. Flexible Delivery Options: While the experiment aimed to simplify delivery options, the results suggest that maintaining flexibility (as in the Non-Food Initiative's multi-tab approach) may be more beneficial for overall revenue.
  1. Market-Specific Strategies: The varying results across different stores in Jordan highlight the need for market-specific strategies, which aligns with the Non-Food Initiative's phased rollout approach across different countries.
  1. User Experience Considerations: Both projects emphasize the importance of a seamless user experience, whether it's through improved product discovery or addressing broken experiences due to product unavailability.

Next Steps

  1. Refine the approach to show available products under different delivery services, excluding only the Scheduled delivery service where necessary.
  1. Incorporate learnings from this experiment into the ongoing rollout of the Non-Food Initiative, especially for markets with similar challenges.
  1. Consider a hybrid approach that maintains the visibility of non-food products while optimizing delivery options based on market-specific needs.
  1. Continue monitoring key metrics across all markets to ensure that efforts to optimize one area (e.g., delivery services) do not negatively impact others (e.g., non-food sales).
